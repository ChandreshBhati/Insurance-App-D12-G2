Terms & Conditions

  • 01
    Waiting Period

    Most policies have a 30-day initial waiting period. Pre-existing diseases (PEDs) typically have a 2–4 year waiting period before coverage kicks in.

  • 02
    Co-Payment Clause

    Some plans require you to pay 10–20% of the claim amount yourself. Co-pay is higher for senior citizens or treatment in non-network hospitals.

  • 03
    Sub-Limits & Room Rent Cap

    Many policies cap room rent at 1–2% of sum insured per day. Exceeding this limit reduces the total reimbursement proportionately.

  • 04
    Exclusions

    Cosmetic treatments, self-inflicted injuries, dental procedures, vision correction, and experimental treatments are typically excluded.

  • 05
    Claim Intimation Window

    For planned hospitalization, intimation must be given 48–72 hours in advance. Emergency claims must be reported within 24 hours of admission.

Commonly Offered Benefits

What You Get

  • No Claim Bonus (NCB)

    Earn 5–50% bonus on your sum insured for every claim-free year, boosting your coverage without extra premium.

  • Network Hospital Cashless

    Access to 5,000–15,000+ network hospitals for zero out-of-pocket cashless treatment — no upfront payment needed.

  • Ambulance Cover

    Emergency ambulance charges up to ₹2,000–₹5,000 per hospitalization covered under most standard plans.

  • Annual Health Checkup

    Free preventive health checkup once per policy year for all insured members — promotes early detection.

  • Tax Benefit (Sec 80D)

    Premium paid qualifies for deduction up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens) under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act.

Before You Buy

Smart Tips

💡

Always choose sum insured ≥ ₹5 lakh given rising medical costs in metro cities.

💡

Opt for plans with restore benefit — replenishes your sum insured if exhausted mid-year.

💡

Check the insurer's Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) — aim for 95%+ for reliable payouts.

💡

Buy health insurance before age 35 to avoid high premiums and PED waiting periods.

💡

Consider a top-up or super top-up plan for enhanced coverage at a much lower cost.
