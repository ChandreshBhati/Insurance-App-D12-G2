Terms & Conditions

  • 01
    Free-Look Period

    A 15–30 day free-look window is mandatory. You can cancel and get a full refund if unsatisfied after reading the policy document.

  • 02
    Suicide Exclusion Clause

    Death by suicide within the first year of policy is excluded. From the 2nd year, 80% of premiums paid or surrender value is returned to nominees.

  • 03
    Grace Period

    A grace period of 15–30 days is provided for premium payment after the due date. The policy remains active during this window.

  • 04
    Medical Disclosure

    All pre-existing conditions must be declared at the time of purchase. Non-disclosure can result in claim rejection even years later.

  • 05
    Policy Lapse & Revival

    If premium is unpaid beyond grace period, the policy lapses. Most insurers allow revival within 2–5 years with back-premium + interest.

Commonly Offered Benefits

What You Get

  • High Coverage at Low Cost

    Get coverage of ₹1 Crore+ for as low as ₹500–₹1,000/month — the most cost-efficient life protection available.

  • Critical Illness Rider

    Add-on rider pays a lump sum on diagnosis of 34–64 critical illnesses like cancer, heart attack, stroke — separate from death benefit.

  • Accidental Death Benefit

    Additional sum assured (1–2x base cover) paid to nominee if death occurs due to an accident under this optional rider.

  • Waiver of Premium Rider

    If the policyholder becomes permanently disabled, future premiums are waived but the policy continues with full coverage.

  • Tax Benefits (80C & 10(10D))

    Premiums up to ₹1.5 lakh are deductible under Sec 80C. Death benefit received by nominee is fully tax-free under Sec 10(10D).

Before You Buy

Smart Tips

💡

Ideal coverage = 10–15× your annual income. Don't underinsure your family's future needs.

💡

Buy term insurance as early as possible — premiums are fixed at purchase age and stay lower lifelong.

💡

Choose cover till age 60–75 to ensure your family is protected through your peak earning and liability years.

💡

Always go for pure term over endowment — returns are not the goal here; protection is.

💡

Add a nominee carefully — name a trustee or use MWP Act to ensure the payout reaches the right person.
